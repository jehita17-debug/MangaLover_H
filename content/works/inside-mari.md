The body-swap premise is a trap. Step past it and what Oshimi Shuzo is actually doing is something far less comfortable: taking the question *what does it mean to be me* and refusing to let it stay abstract. The horror here is not supernatural. It is the slow recognition that the self you have been treating as a fixed thing — your desires, your self-loathing, the story you tell about why you are the way you are — might be a habit more than a truth.

The dread arrives before anything overtly frightening happens. It lives in the negative space between what the characters say and what they are doing. Oshimi draws stillness the way other artists draw action, and that stillness accumulates weight across chapters until ordinary domestic details feel unbearable.

This is not a mystery to be solved. There is no clean revelation waiting to make the discomfort retroactively earned. The book is interested in people who want, desperately, to stop being themselves — and in what that wish costs the people around them.

Skip it if you need your psychological fiction to end with someone healed. Skip it if the prospect of spending nine volumes inside a genuinely unreliable consciousness sounds exhausting rather than necessary. Read it if you have ever wanted to disappear into someone else's life and hated yourself for wanting that.

<!--SPOILER-->
The reveal that Mari and Isao may be facets of a single fractured consciousness reframes every prior interaction as a kind of internal argument rather than an external relationship. What reads initially as voyeurism — and Oshimi does not let the reader off the hook for finding it voyeuristic — becomes a portrait of dissociation so complete that the self has had to invent an outside observer just to survive. The final volumes' insistence on staying inside that ambiguity rather than resolving it is either the most honest move the book could make or a refusal to do the harder work, depending on how much you needed the ending to declare something true. I have gone back and forth on that. I still have not decided.
